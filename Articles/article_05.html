Walking in Brussels, Belgium

2022
2 min read

As I stroll through the streets of Brussels, modern minimalist buildings stand intertwined with classical facades marked by the passage of time, as if past and present whisper and converge here.

Slanting sunlight pierces the shadows, dancing across the cobblestones and tram tracks, transforming the clamor of the world into a warm, serene golden glow.

At the end of the street, the majestic Église Royale Sainte-Marie soars into the sky, its massive dome rendered solemn and sacred by the play of light and shadow, like a pearl embedded in the heart of Brussels. The tram tracks wind off into the distance, not only connecting the city's transit but also serving as a temporal pathway, guiding one toward the centuries-old dome that stands resolute.
